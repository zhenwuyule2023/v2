Global Perspectives on Mental Health

Mental health is an essential part of peoples lives and society. Poor mental health affects our well-being, our ability to work, and our relationships with friends, family, and community.

Mental health conditions are not uncommon. Hundreds of millions suffer from them yearly, and many more do over their lifetimes. It’s estimated that 1 in 3 women and 1 in 5 men will experience major depression in their lives. Other conditions, such as schizophrenia and bipolar disorder, are less common but still have a large impact on people’s lives.

Burden of Disease from Each Category of Mental Illness (2019)

The charts provide insights into the distribution of mental illness burden. The pie chart showcases the comparative impact of each type, while the bar graph presents the absolute DALYs per 100,000 people for each illness type. DALY, or Disability-Adjusted Life Year, represents the total years of healthy life lost due to a disease.

Global Anxiety Disorders Prevalence (2019)

This geographical chart paints a global view of anxiety disorder prevalence. Darker shades indicate higher prevalence rates. With its detailed granularity at a country level, the visualization offers an understanding of the geographical distribution and impact of anxiety disorders.
